Nasa Spacesuit

Professional Project for Covestro LLC

Background

This project was completed in my second co-op rotation at Covestro LLC. Covestro specializes in polymers, and I worked in the business unit tailored specifically to polycarbonates. In this business unit, I worked under the application development team to analyze the viability of polycarbonates within new applications. For this project, I was responsible for analyzing NASA space suits for future use. The aim of this project was to test the feasibility of polycarbonates and composites within the torso and briefs of space suits.

Pictures

Unfortunately, this project is on-going, so pictures and results are not available to be shared.

Details

For this project, I analyzed the performance of high-strength polycarbonates/composites within NASA space suits. In particular, I focused mostly on the briefs, with a little work on the torso. In the past, the structural elements of the suit were fairly heavy, usually made of fiberglass and metal. The material selection results in a suit that is very heavy (typically over 200 lbs) and stiff. NASA wanted to test the viability of polycarbonates, which weigh significantly less than metals. To analyze the feasibility, I set up a structural analysis of the suit using given boundary conditions (such as pressures). After completion, I identified locations that needed more/less material to satisfy the desired criteria. I reported the results and design suggestions back to NASA.
