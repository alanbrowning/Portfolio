Rethink the Rink

Professional Project for Covestro LLC

Background

This project was completed in my second co-op rotation at Covestro LLC. Covestro specializes in polymers, and I worked in the business unit tailored specifically to polycarbonates. In this business unit, I worked under the application development team to analyze the viability of polycarbonates within new applications. The Rethink the Rink project is a joint effort between Covestro, Carnegie Mellon University, and the Pittsburgh Penguins. Each year, students will propose designs for various hockey equipment to increase safety in the sport. For this project, I was responsible for analyzing the performannce of a board proposal based off of a real-life collision.

Pictures

Unfortunately, this project is on-going, so pictures and results are not available to be shared.


Details

In this project, I was given the pleasure of working with the Penguins. Previously, a board design was proposed which utilized polycarbonate to reduce decelerations within the boards. This reduction was proposed to help minimize the chance of injury on collision. Early in my rotation, a Penguins player was severely injuried due to a harsh collision. To show the possible benefits of the new design, I was tasked with creating a simulation which would mimic that of the injury.

To create the simulation, I first had to establish boundary conditions. Using frame data from the collision and video analyzing software, I was able to back out an impact velocity of the player. Using a scale human CAD model, I was able to apply the impact velocity. I then analyzed the maximum stresses and decelerations due to collision for both the existing boards and the proposed ones. We were able to determine that new boards created a significant reduction in maximum decelerations, as the board was designed to do. The results were then presented to the Penguins, and the boards are currently being implemented within their practice facilities.
