Rethink the Rink

Professional Project for Covestro LLC

Background

This project was completed in my second co-op rotation at Covestro LLC. The Rethink the Rink project is a joint effort between Covestro, Carnegie Mellon University, and the Pittsburgh Penguins. Each year, students at CMU propose designs for various hockey equipment to increase safety in the sport. One student proposal had been accepted by Covestro for review. The proposal was for a design that would help reduce injuries during collisions with the board. I was responsible for analyzing the performannce of a board proposal based off of a real-life collision/injury.

The proposed board utilized polycarbonate to reduce decelerations within the boards. Physical testing of the boards was already taking place, but early in my rotation, a Penguins player (Jake Guentzel) was severely injuried due to a harsh collision. To demonstrate benefits of the new design, I was tasked with creating a simulation which would mimic that collision injury, and show the quantitative reduction in deceleration using the new board design.

To create the simulation, I first had to establish boundary conditions. Using frame data from the collision and video analyzing software, I was able to back out an impact velocity of the player. Using a scale human CAD model, I was able to apply the impact velocity. I had to run an explicit model, which was better suited to the transient, nonlinear, high deflection polycarbonate boards. I then analyzed the maximum stresses and decelerations due to collision for both the existing boards and the proposed ones. We were able to determine that new boards created a significant reduction in maximum decelerations, as the board was designed to do. The results were then presented to the Penguins, and the boards are currently being implemented within their practice facilities.
