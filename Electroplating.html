Electroplating Agitation Device

Professional Project for Collins Aerospace

Details

This project started on August of 2021 at Collins Aerospace and is currently ongoing. For this project, I was the lead mechanical engineer investigating low yields for electroplating of RF filters.

Collins Aerospace is a global leader in the production of small RF filters. These filters are critical to the efficacy of small form factor radios. An important step in the production of these filters is gold electroplating. The reliability in this step was found to be lower than acceptable. I was tasked with analyzing the issue and providing a resolution to increase yields.

In collaboration with chemical engineers, I determined that the root cause of the poor reliability of the plating was small crevices in the filters on the wafer (located at the cathode). The narrow entrances would plate first and prevent plating within the crevice. To fix this issue, I concluded move the wafer to induce fluid flow into the crevice. This would better allow for gold particles into these areas. I designed a linear motion device that would oscillate the wafer within the solution. The device consisted of stepper motors connected to a camshaft and slotted block. The cam would linearly oscillate the blocks, which would drive the motion of the wafer holder. Prototype models were 3D printed and showed success in increasing plating yields. Full-scale production of the device is currently ongoing.
