ASME Human Powered Vehicle Challenge

Extracurricular Project for Human Powered Vehicle Club

Details

This project was completed as a part of the human powered vehicle club. The goal of the project was to design a recumbent bicycle to complete in the ASME Human Powered Vehicle Challenge.

The ASME Human Powered Vehicle Challenge is an opportunity to use applicable engineering principles to design a human powered vehicle. Typically, the vehicle is a recumbent bicycle. The challenge was graded on 3 criteria: a speed event, an endurance event, and a design report. There are a few nuances that involved the design criteria, but generally it was fairly relaxed. Most of my work revolved the design of the bicycle frame. The frame is an integral part of the bicycle, as it creates a level of safety for the rider. This frame is shown in the pictures above. To design the cage, we researched the size of the particpants, and made the cage slightly larger. To verify the safety of the cage, we created a finite element analysis via ANSYS to determine the maximum deformation. We estimated impact forces from rolling over, and then we applied the forces in a static structural analysis in ANSYS. The results are shown in the pictures below. The results validated our design, as they were below the allowable deformation via the ASME rules.

This sort of analysis was repeated for other custom made components, like the tray, main boom, and seat. Additionally, a cost analysis was conducted. In the end, the bicycle competed in competition at ASME: East at Penn State University.
