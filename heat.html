Quantifying the Performance of a Window AC Unit

Academic Project at the University of Pittsburgh

Details

This project was completed as a part of my Mechanical Measurements II course at the University of Pittsburgh. The goal of this project was to design a test setup and procedure to determine the coefficient of performance of a window AC Unit. Due to Covid-19, this project consisted only of a report and presentation.

This project was completed in the summer of 2020, when it was hot in Pittsburgh. Most student houses did not have central AC, so many relied on a window AC unit. As such, I decided to create a way of measuring the coefficient of performance, which then could be compared to the manufacturer's given value. I determined two different ways of accomplishing this task.

The coefficient of performance is defined as the rate of heat flow into the evaporator divided by the work input (via electricity). Measuring the electrical input is rather simple with a multimeter. There are a few ways to determine heat transfer.

The first way would be to measure the air properties. Enthlapy can be determined (via temperature and pressure sensors) at both the inlet and outlet of the AC unit. Mass flow rate is then measured using a flow meter. The only nuance to this method is that air carries moisture. This moisture plays a substantial role in the heat transfer, so humidity has to be accounted for when using this method.

The second method would be to measure the refrigerant properties. This is slightly more difficult, as the AC unit would need to be disassembled to install the transducers. This method would also be more expensive, as the sensors would have to withstand higher pressures and be capable of measuring refrigerants.

To validate these designs, I performed an uncertainty analysis on both. Due to the error in the desired sensors, the airflow method had uncertainty of about 9%, whereas the refrigerant had about 3.5%.

I proposed that given the correct budget, the refrigerant method would be optimal. I also created a test procedure that documents how to actually test the coefficient of performance once the test is set up.
